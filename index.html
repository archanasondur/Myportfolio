My Story

I run on organized chaos. I respect systems because life changes fast, so I build lightweight ones that can bend without breaking. I like clarity, but I’m not attached to perfect plans. If priorities shift, I shift faster. Give me a messy problem and a moving target, and I’ll still make progress look predictable.

I don’t chase leadership titles, but I do chase ownership. If something is mine, it’s getting done and getting done well. I work best with autonomy and clear goals, and I ask for help early when I’m blocked. No ego, just momentum.

Autonomy doesn’t mean I’m a solo act. I love teams when the goal is shared and communication is real. I’m not the loudest voice in the room because I prefer listening first and speaking with intention. I’ll connect dots, surface risks early, and give an opinion that moves decisions forward.

Fun fact: I can start with scattered notes, 20 browser tabs, and a half-formed idea, and end with a clean plan, clear priorities, and a test list. It’s oddly satisfying.

In crunch time, I’m a “less talk, more ship” person. Outside the rush, I’m all for a friendly, positive environment where people feel safe to ask questions and do their best work.
